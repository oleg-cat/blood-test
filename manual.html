About the task

In this task you will see photos of human blood cells. Your mission is to determine a type of cell in the image.

Criteria for choosing the type of cell

To select the correct type, you need to rely on the instructions and examples below. You only need to choose one option for each cell.

# Name Description Schema Example 1 Example 2
1 Eosinophil The cell nucleus is divided into two parts by a pinch in the middle. No other cells has such feature. Red and orange dots (grains) are visible in the pink layer.
2 Lymphocyte They have a round or oval large cell nucleus and no granularity. They are small, and there is almost no cytoplasm - the main content of the cell.
3 Monocyte The cell nucleus is not divided into fragments. It is also quite large, dark, slightly elongated, and looks like a bean. White grains - lysosomes - may be visible as well.
4 Neutrophil The cell nucleus is divided into several (2-4) unequal parts connected by pinches. Granularity is well expressed within the pink layer.
